I'm not diagnosably manic or even hypomanic but I have felt the need to google
the diagnostic criteria for cyclothymia from time to time in my life. I don't
think this is even that, I'm just having a lot of fun.

I think I mentioned before that I have a hard time even speculating on what it
would be like to try to 'vibecode' without already knowing a lot about writing
code and about how computers work. I've had the chance to watch one person do
it and it wasn't terrifying or anything but he's also one of the smartest
people I know and a math major so it's probably not a representative sample.

Anyway, from over here as a Pretty Good™ programmer this new era is pretty fun
so far. I'm counting the "new era" as being roughly from CC 4.5 to present. I
can see what Casey Muratori (for instance) means when he says he's just not
interested at all, and that to him the whole point is to figure out *how* to do
things and not just to get them done, but I have to say I detect a slight edge
of derision there that I don't think is necessary. I was a nerdy kid but I
wasn't drawn into programming for its own sake. I get the compulsion sometimes,
the one that Feynman describes in his Los Alamos lectures, to just make the
machine do something, and then see if you can make it do the next slightly more
impressive thing after that, and so on; but it's not my main motivation and I
don't think that makes me lesser than. I have ideas for things that I want to
do, that I want to see finished, and it's not that I don't care at all about
how they're done, I care about the quality of the code and the correctness of
the process, but my primary motivation is to see the finished thing. My
secondary motivation is to make enough money from at least one of the things
that I can keep working on exactly whatever next one I want to without anyone
else's permission or even participation, and the third is to end up with a
perfectly polished artifact.

Prior to this era I could get a lot done I think, by way of being a generalist
and reasonably smart, and a decent typist, and having some imagination; but the
distance from idea to prototype to refinement is so short now that it's almost
addictive and I don't know exactly what it is that I'm doing, or what it is
about me intrinsically that's different from other people, such that I read and
hear in person so often from people who hate the new way of doing things, or
are so thoroughly uninterested that they haven't even tried the new tools out.

The most similar experience to my own that I've heard so far was DHH on [a
podcast recently](https://youtube.com/watch?v=JiWgKRgdgpI) saying that (in
short) he thought for a while 'This isn't what I signed up for I don't want to
be a project manager of agents', and then suddenly it went from feeling like a
management job to a 'mechsuit' he can step into and work ten times faster with
his hands in the code in ten places at once. It really does kind of feel like
that.

I haven't shipped much of anything (with users) yet from the mechsuit so maybe
my opinions aren't worth all that much, but I was able to take my decade-long
personal event-sourcing database library and rewrite almost all of it, with
more tests and better documentation than I ever had before, and add a whole
multi-tenant orchestration layer to it, and all kind of other features, which a
small but non-zero number of alpha testers are putting into actual production
now, all in about 3 months. The product is [j17.dev](https://www.j17.dev) btw.
If you want to try it out let me know and I'll give you an alpha signup code.
Or try to break in and send me a security disclosure if you find something I
missed.

Meanwhile I keep having ideas from years past resurface, and between the new
tools and having the database on hand (and in need of early production apps to
pressure test it) I'm building almost all of them. I need to write a couple of
more blog posts about [Frike](/blog/frike-origin), but in the mean time I've
started work on a scheduling app for small businesses, a slightly unhinged
training platform for people who want to learn practically useless skills like
gameboy and gameboy color assembly language, and revisited a couple of mostly
abandoned projects like my [ambient audio
mixer](https://github.com/whiskeytuesday/atmora), and improved this site and
[office hours dot lol](https://www.officehours.lol), and felt like I had time
to write this blog on top of that. Things that never seemed worth doing when
they'd take a week or two to even prototype seem tractable when it takes a day
or two instead.

I suppose I should be worried that the craft is over and the easy money is
going away but I never got one of those jobs (I probably should have) anyway.
Anyway, go build something, and use my database if you want, and check back
here for more rambling incoherent blog posts (not written by AI) soon. This one
wasn't even [retyped](/blog/retyping).
